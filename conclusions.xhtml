Conclusions

There are a multitude of conclusions that could be drawn from the charts that follow, and that was my intention with this project: to create data that would be usable far beyond my specific project. Thus I am not going to write a long attempt at analyzing everything that could be analzyed, not only because there is far more here than I could find, but also because, as this site has illustrated, the interpretation of the facts is rife with contreversy, so even with numbers, any conclusion drawn should be held very lightly. However, a couple things that I do see as being quite obvious: Jewish men do most of the talking, and most of the existing. This is unsurprising, seeing as all the books analyzed were written by Jewish men, but it is nonetheless an overwhelming bias.Secondly, the statistics on class are far less compelling than those on ethnicity and gender because for more than half of the characters, their class is unknown.
