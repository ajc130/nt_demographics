Gathering Data

In various research projects I have undertaken, I have often noticed a distinct lack of statistics regarding the demographic data of the characters in the new testament. For example, if I am going to make a statement like "Relative to the culture in which it was written, the Bible has a lot of female characters", I would like to be able to back that up by providing the percentage of characters who are female, but I was unsuccessful in finding that percentage. Thus, the primary purpose of this site is to give you the statistics I have managed to produce: things like the percentage of characters who are woman and who are Jewish and who are poor, and the percentage of the speaking that is done by men verses women. For more on that, see the conclusions page.

The other half of this puzzle for me was the way that objectively, there are numbers that can be found, and I found them, but that doesn't change the fact that Christians always have and probably always will disagree wildly on issues related to demographics, such as the role of women in the church. Thus I decided to look at the interpretations done by the two largest denominations in the United States on all of the passages in the New Testament in which instruction are given for a specific group. A summary of thoe findings are on this site as well.
